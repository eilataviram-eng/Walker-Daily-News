SASSA eLife Glitch Raises Concerns Over Grant Payments

By Walker Daily News Staff • Published

A reported technical issue affecting the South African Social Security Agency’s (SASSA) eLife certification system has raised concerns among grant recipients about potential disruptions to their payments. While officials have acknowledged system challenges, they have sought to reassure beneficiaries that investigations are underway and that updates will be provided as more information becomes available.

The eLife certification process is used by SASSA to confirm that certain grant beneficiaries—particularly those who may not regularly access services in person—are still eligible to receive payments. This digital verification system plays a key role in preventing fraud and ensuring that grants are paid to the correct individuals.

According to SASSA, the current glitch has affected parts of this certification system, leading to delays or difficulties for some users attempting to complete their eLife verification. Officials said the issue appears to be technical in nature, though they have not provided detailed information on the root cause.

At this stage, it remains unclear exactly how many beneficiaries are affected. However, reports indicate that individuals who rely on remote or digital verification methods may be more likely to experience problems. This includes recipients who are required to periodically confirm their status through the eLife system to continue receiving payments.

For affected individuals, the primary concern is whether the glitch could interrupt their grant payments. SASSA has stated that it is aware of these concerns and is working to resolve the issue as quickly as possible. Officials have not confirmed widespread payment suspensions but have advised beneficiaries to remain attentive to official communication channels.

SASSA has encouraged recipients who are experiencing difficulties with eLife certification to avoid panic and to follow updates only from its verified platforms. These include the agency’s official website, its verified social media accounts on platforms such as Facebook and X (formerly Twitter), and direct SMS notifications sent to registered beneficiaries. Local SASSA offices and call centres are also considered reliable sources of information, particularly for individuals who may not have regular internet access.

Beneficiaries are advised to:

  • Monitor announcements from SASSA through its official website and verified social media pages
  • Check for SMS updates sent directly to their registered mobile numbers
  • Attempt certification again once the system stabilises
  • Visit local SASSA offices or contact the call centre if urgent assistance is needed, especially if certification deadlines are approaching

Officials added that contingency measures may be considered if the issue persists, though no specific interventions have yet been announced.

For many recipients, the uncertainty has created anxiety about the reliability of upcoming payments. However, SASSA has emphasized that resolving the technical fault is a priority and that efforts are being made to minimise disruption.

Looking ahead, beneficiaries are encouraged to stay informed through these verified channels and to act promptly if they receive instructions regarding their certification status. Further updates are expected as the agency continues to assess the scope of the problem and implement fixes.

Authorities have indicated that additional communication will be issued once the system is fully restored or if any changes to payment processes are required.
